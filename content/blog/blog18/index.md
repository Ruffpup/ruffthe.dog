There’s a moment where something shifts.

Not loudly. Not all at once. Quietly enough that you almost miss it, until you realize you are not the same person who walked into the room a year ago.

Last year, I came to Spring Training and started rediscovering something I had buried for a long time. **Play. Connection. Curiosity. Joy.**

Ruff did not suddenly appear in my life. I did not create him. **I rediscovered him.**

And over the past year, I stopped treating that as something small or private and started letting it be something real. Something visible. Something shared.

Somewhere along the way, without really planning it, things started to change. I started teaching. I started showing up more consistently. I started connecting with pups not just in Atlanta, but in London, Montreal, Sydney, and beyond. I watched people walk into their first spaces unsure if they belonged, and then soften into themselves when they realized they did.

That is when it became clear **this was never just about me.**

So I am doing something that feels both exciting and a little vulnerable to say out loud.

**I am running for Georgia Pup 2026.**

Not because I need a title, but because I believe in what visibility can do when it is rooted in care. I have seen what happens when someone feels safe enough to be fully themselves for the first time. I know what it feels like to shrink yourself to fit into a space, and what it feels like to finally stop doing that.

Over the past year, I have used the phrase **#UnapologeticallyPuppy** a lot. It is not about being loud or rebellious. It is not about taking up space at the expense of others. It is about being fully yourself without asking permission.

It means showing up with joy even when it feels vulnerable. It means leading with consent, communication, and care. It means creating space so others feel safe doing the same.

Being unapologetically puppy is not about standing out. It is about making sure no one feels like they have to hide.

Alongside this announcement, I am also launching my platform on my site. It goes deeper into the work I care about, but at its core it is simple. **Visibility. Connection. Care.**

Helping people be seen as they are. Bridging communities and experiences. Building spaces that are intentional, safe, and sustainable.

Why now is a question I have asked myself more than once.

The answer is not because everything is perfect. It is because **I trust myself in a way I did not before.** I have done the work to understand who I am. I have stopped asking for permission to exist.

A year ago, I came back to the place where I found Ruff and realized something I did not expect.

**Last year was about rediscovering Ruff. This year, I stopped leaving him behind and finally brought him with me.**

If you see me out in the world, come say hi. Come connect. This was never meant to be a solo journey.

And now it is time to take the next step.

— Ruff
